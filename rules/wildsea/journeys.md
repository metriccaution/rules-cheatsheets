# Departure

- Needs a ship - your own, or borrowed
- Pick a destination
- Make preparation
  - E.g. Hiring extra crew, cargo & passengers, planning
- The Firefly creates **journey tracks**
  - **Hidden tracks**
  - **Progress** - Only when the **Progress track** is filled do you arrive
  - **Risk** - When you're travelling through particularly risky locations, track it with a risk track
  - **Pathfinding** - See Cartographizing below
  - **Riot** - Tracks crew / passenger dissatisfaction, rarely used

# Progress

- Player role depends on their station

## At the helm

- **Cut a path**
  - Traveling normally
  - Mark a single box on the progress track
  - Usually have a choice about encountering things spotted on watch
- **Forge ahead**
  - Going fast
  - Mark two boxes on the progress track
  - Usually have to encounter what's spotted on watch; the ship may take damage if you try to avoid
- **Drop anchor**
  - Stop
  - Do a montage
  - No watch roll is made - the undercrew keeps watch instead
- Challenging terrain may require an additional **ratings roll** at the helm

## On watch

- Two options: make a watch roll or spend resources for a discovery.
- Watch roll
  - Roll a single D6
  - Firefly rolls a D6 **threat roll** (hidden from players)
    - If you own a chart of the area, the Firefly rolls 2d6 and takes the higher result
  - Threat roll results:
    - 6 - Danger-free opportunity
    - 4-5 - Hazardous encounter with a payoff
    - 1-3 - Immediate danger with little chance of payoff
  - Consult the Watch Roll Results table
- Discovery
  - Spend a **chart** _and_ a **whisper** - both are lost
  - Gives the player input into a discovered location
    - The location has to be something that might be recorded on a map or chart
  - Firefly still makes a hidden threat roll

### Encounters

- Avoiding
  - You may need to make a **ratings roll** to avoid an encounter if it's something that can give chase
  - Harder if you're forging ahead
- Engage
  - Firefly should give some indication of threat
  - Choice, challenge, or scene
    - Choice
      - Outcome of the encounter depends on a choice
      - E.g. do you investigate an emergency flare or not
    - Challenge
      - Some kind of threat to the ship
      - Player at the helm makes a ratings roll, and go from there
      - E.g. A pirate ship opens fire
    - Scene
      - Usually involves leaving the ship
      - E.g. exploring a ruin

## Tending the engine

- Proceed normally
- You can hijack focus for engine trouble
- Overload the engine
  - Spend a resource as fuel
  - Increase impact relating to ship speed, or mark an extra box for progress
- Muffle the engine
  - Uses a resource to muffle the engine
  - Increase impact for stealth, or stealthily leave if you haven't been spotted yet

## Cartographizing

- Firefly makes a track
- Mark it when you find an important landmark
- Gain a related chart when you complete the track

## Watching the Weather

- Similar to being on watch
- But you always roll
- Check the Weather-Watching Results table

# Arrival

- When the progress track is filled
- Firefly describes where you've arrived
- Spend some time port-side to heal, relax, and repair the ship
