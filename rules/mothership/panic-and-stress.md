---
title: Panic and Stress
reference:
  - Player's Survival Guide, page 20
  - Player's Survival Guide, page 21
---

- The more **stress** you have, the more likely you are to panic when bad things happen
- Keep track of your **stress** (you start with 2)
  - You have a minimum **stress** and can't go below this
  - Maximum **stress** is 20; any stress taken over 20 reduces the most relevant stat or save instead
  - To relieve stress, make a **Rest Save** using your **worst** save
    - Success: reduce stress by the ones digit of the roll
    - Failure: gain 1 stress
    - Leisure activities give **advantage**; unsafe locations give **disadvantage**
- Make a **panic check** whenever
  - You roll a **critical failure**
  - Dramatically appropriate events (e.g. witnessing death, encountering strange entities)
  - Whenever you choose to
- When you need to make a **panic check**, roll a d20
  - Greater than your stress - Success
  - Equal to or under - Failure, consult the panic table
