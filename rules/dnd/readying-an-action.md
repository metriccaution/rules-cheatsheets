---
title: Readying an Action
reference: Player's Handbook, page 193
---

1. Use your **action** to ready another action
2. Declare a **trigger** — a perceivable circumstance ("when the enemy moves into the doorway")
3. Declare a **response** — what you will do when the trigger occurs
4. Your **reaction** is held in reserve until the trigger fires
5. When the trigger occurs you can use your **reaction** to take the readied action, or ignore it
   1. If the trigger doesn't occur before your next turn, the readied action is lost

# Readying a Spell

- You can ready a spell as normal, but the spell must have a casting time of **1 action**
- Expend the spell slot on your turn when readying
- You hold the spell's energy with concentration until the trigger occurs
  - If your concentration is broken, the spell is lost without effect
  - You cannot maintain concentration on another spell while holding a readied spell
- When the trigger occurs, use your reaction to release the spell
- If the trigger doesn't occur before your next turn, the spell is lost without effect
